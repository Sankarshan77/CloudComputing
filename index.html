Architecture

IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS

The "Service Stack"

IaaS: Raw infrastructure (VMs, Storage). You manage OS, Apps, Data. (e.g., EC2, GCE)

PaaS: Platform for devs. Provider manages OS/Middleware; you manage Apps/Data. (e.g., Heroku, App Engine)

SaaS: Complete software. Managed 100% by provider. (e.g., Gmail, Salesforce)

Finance

CapEx vs OpEx

Cloud Financial Models

CapEx (Capital Expenditure): Large upfront investment in physical hardware. Depreciates over time.

OpEx (Operational Expenditure): Pay-as-you-go. No upfront cost. Treated as a day-to-day business expense.

Benefit: Cloud shifts IT costs from CapEx to OpEx.

Security

Shared Responsibility

Who secures what?

Provider (Cloud): Responsible for security OF the cloud (Physical DC, Hardware, Hypervisor).

Customer (User): Responsible for security IN the cloud (Patching Guest OS, IAM, Data Encryption, Firewalls).
